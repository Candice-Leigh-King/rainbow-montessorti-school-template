4. How can I tell if a school is truly Montessori certified?


The term "Montessori" is not legally protected, meaning any school can use it and claim to be Montessori, regardless of adherence to authentic Montessori principles.

Rainbow Montessori School is an authentic Montessori school led by MACTE accredited trained teachers, certified materials, and a philosophy rooted in independence, self-directed learning, and respect for the child. Our schools also follow Ontario Ministry of Education standards, while also offering enriched learning that often helps children progress well beyond age-level expectations—academically, socially, and emotionally.

To identify a genuine Montessori school, parents should look for:

  • Certified Montessori educators (e.g., AMI, MACTE)

  • Montessori-specific materials

  • Mixed-age classrooms and smaller, more personalized class sizes with low student to teacher ratio

  • A calm, structured, child-led environment

We encourage families to ask questions and observe classrooms when choosing a Montessori program.

5. Who is Montessori for?


Our program is best suited for those who are seeking a dedicated Montessori-based education and philosophy, and who value a long-term, child-centered approach to learning—which ultimately benefits their child’s educational journey.

Montessori is especially beneficial for children who thrive in:

  • Structured independence

  • Hands-on, self-paced learning

  • Calm, focused environments

  • Smaller, more personalized class sizes with low student to teacher ratio - allows us to truly support each child’s individual journey and build the foundation for a lifetime of confident, engaged learning.

This approach nurtures confidence, curiosity, and concentration—skills that lay a strong foundation for enriched lifelong learning and often helps children progress well beyond age-level expectations—academically, socially, and emotionally.

6. Is Rainbow Montessori School part of the Canada-Wide Early Learning and Child Care reduced fee government program?


While we have opted into the program, Rainbow Montessori is not part of the CWELCC program. The reason for this is that the CWELCC program is currently at capacity due to limited government funding, and we are not eligible to participate until further funding is allocated. Once additional government funding becomes available again and our opt in application is accepted, we will update this section accordingly!

7. Do you offer a subsidized program?


No, Rainbow Montessori does not offer a subsidized program. As a specialized private school offering an enriched Montessori education with a unique, hands-on approach to early learning, our fees are set independently and are not covered by publicly funded subsidy models.
